Overview Executive Summary

Ambient-noise HVSR measurements at 14 microzonation sites across Yangon reveal fundamental frequencies of 0.6–1.5 Hz (periods 0.65–1.67 s), consistent with deep alluvial deposits. All 44 analysed townships are classified as NEHRP Site Class E (Very Soft Soil). Ground-motion recordings from the M4.2 event show PGA values of 0.6–101 cm/s² at epicentral distances of 3–25 km. North Okkalapa Township carries the highest composite seismic risk index (SRI = 0.80), driven by elevated hazard amplification and a large building stock.

Data quality note: 10 of 25 microzonation sites lack a vertical (EHZ) channel and could not be processed for HVSR. Station T9951 was excluded from ground-motion analysis due to instrument-response mismatch. Event-window detection relied on peak-amplitude fallback for most stations; only S7E9A provided a robust STA/LTA trigger at 07:02 UTC.

Module 1 HVSR Site Characterization

Horizontal-to-Vertical Spectral Ratio (HVSR) analysis was performed on 25 ambient-noise recordings collected at SP01–SP25 (no SP22) using Raspberry Shake 3-component sensors. 14 sites returned valid results; 11 were excluded due to missing EHZ (vertical) channels. Processing used 30-second Hanning-tapered windows with Konno-Ohmachi smoothing (b = 40) and a transient-rejection threshold (RMS > 4× median discarded).

Module 2 Spatial Microzonation

HVSR results from the 14 valid sites were spatially interpolated to a 0.005° grid covering all 44 townships using Ordinary Kriging (linear variogram model) with IDW fallback. Each township was assigned a NEHRP site class based on the interpolated fundamental period T₀. All townships classify as NEHRP Site Class E (Very Soft Soil), reflecting Yangon's deep Holocene alluvial basin.

Uniform NEHRP-E classification: The absence of bedrock or stiff-soil reference sites within the measurement network means spatial discrimination is limited to variations within Class E. Measured T₀ values range from 0.81 s (Hlegu) to 1.38 s (Sanchaung), representing meaningful differences in depth-to-bedrock and sediment stiffness across the city, even within the same NEHRP class.

Module 3 Earthquake Ground Motion

Waveform data from the M4.2 Yangon earthquake (22 May 2026) were processed at 6 Raspberry Shake stations in the Yangon metropolitan area. Instrument responses were removed to obtain acceleration time series; PGA, PGV, and Arias Intensity were computed from the horizontal components. Station T9951 was excluded (instrument-response mismatch; physically impossible PGA computed). Event-window detection used STA/LTA (1 s / 10 s); most stations fell back to peak-amplitude detection on the full-day trace.

Event time uncertainty: Only station S7E9A produced a robust STA/LTA trigger at 07:02 UTC on 22 May 2026, consistent with the expected M4.2 event. R7183 triggered at 23:43 UTC — this may reflect a separate local event or elevated noise. PGA values from R8939, RDBC5, and RF99C (fallback detection) should be treated as upper-bound estimates for the M4.2 event at those sites.

Module 4 Site Amplification — SSR vs. HVSR

Standard Spectral Ratio (SSR) amplification was computed for each event station using S7E9A as the reference (most distant station, lowest expected site amplification). SSR fundamental frequencies are compared against the nearest HVSR measurement to assess consistency between the ambient-noise and earthquake-based methods.

Reference station caveat: SSR ideally requires a bedrock reference station. S7E9A is a soft-soil station (~25 km from epicentre) used here as the best available substitute. All computed SSR A₀ values are therefore relative amplifications, not absolute site factors.

Module 5 Building Exposure & Vulnerability

639,318 building footprints from OpenStreetMap-derived data were spatially joined to the 44 township boundaries. Building density, coverage ratio, and footprint statistics were computed per township. A resonance risk proxy was derived by comparing inferred building natural periods (estimated from footprint area) against the site T₀ from Module 2.

Resonance proxy limitation: Building natural period was estimated from footprint area using an empirical size proxy (T_bldg ≈ 0.1√area/10). Without actual storey counts or structural type data, all townships return "Low" resonance risk — the proxy is conservative. Real resonance assessment would require storey-count or height data.

Module 6 Integrated Seismic Risk Map

A composite Seismic Risk Index (SRI) was computed for each township by combining three normalised sub-scores: site hazard (H, weight 0.40), building exposure (E, weight 0.35), and recorded PGA (P, weight 0.25). SRI = 0.40·H + 0.35·E + 0.25·P. All sub-scores were normalised to [0, 1] across the 44-township dataset.

Methods Data & Methodology

HVSR (Module 1)

30-second Hanning-tapered windows; max 150 windows; transient rejection at 4× median RMS(Z). Konno-Ohmachi smoothing b = 40. Geometric mean H/V; f₀ searched 0.5–12 Hz. Instrument response removed with ObsPy (output = VEL, pre_filt = 0.1/0.2/45/50 Hz). 3-stage time-window fallback: stated UTC → local (UTC+6:30) → full recording +30 min.

Spatial Interpolation (Module 2)

Ordinary Kriging (PyKrige, linear variogram) on a 0.005° grid. IDW fallback if Kriging fails. NEHRP class assigned from interpolated T₀: T₀ > 0.5 s → Class E (Very Soft).

Ground Motion (Module 3)

MiniSEED + StationXML loaded with ObsPy. Channel selection: EHZ + EHE/EHN if available, else EHZ + ENE/ENN. Response removed → acceleration (output = ACC, pre_filt = 0.1/0.2/45/50 Hz, water_level = 60). PGA from geometric mean of horizontal peak absolute acceleration. Arias Intensity via np.trapezoid.

SSR Amplification (Module 4)

Standard Spectral Ratio vs. S7E9A reference. 5 s pre-signal, 30 s signal window. Konno-Ohmachi smoothing applied to Fourier spectra. f₀ identified as peak of geometric-mean horizontal SSR.

Building Exposure (Module 5)

639,318 building footprints (GeoJSON, ~247 MB) projected to UTM Zone 47N, area computed, spatially joined to townships. Density = buildings/km². Coverage = sum(footprint)/township area. Resonance proxy: T_bldg estimated from footprint area.

Risk Index (Module 6)

SRI = 0.40·H + 0.35·E + 0.25·P. Hazard score from normalised mean_A0×f0 product. Exposure score from normalised building density. PGA score from IDW-interpolated station PGA at township centroids, normalised. Classes: Very Low <0.20, Low 0.20–0.45, Moderate 0.45–0.70, High ≥ 0.70.

Known Limitations & Caveats

  • 10 microzonation sites lack EHZ — spatial coverage is uneven (southern townships under-represented)
  • SP01 A₀ = 148 is anomalously high and excluded from hazard scoring
  • All NEHRP classes are E — no bedrock reference available to resolve sub-class differences
  • T9951 excluded from all quantitative analysis (instrument-response mismatch)
  • R7183 trigger at 23:43 UTC may not correspond to the M4.2 event (07:02 UTC per S7E9A)
  • Resonance risk is conservative (footprint proxy); height/storey data needed for accurate building-period estimation
  • SRI weights (0.40 / 0.35 / 0.25) are expert-judgement assignments, not calibrated against loss data
